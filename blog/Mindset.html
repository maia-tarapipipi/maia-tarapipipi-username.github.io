Neuroplasticity

Question: Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

Understanding that your brain can change throughout your life, you can learn and grow new skills. Which is useful for people of any age that their ability isn't predetermined or static but can change at any age.

Challenging your brain with new activities and learning new skills can improve your brain function and strengthen the connections in your brain.

Neuroplasticity can help create new thought pathways, which is useful for people who struggle with anxiety and depression in creating new ways of thinking and creating new habits.

Principles of neuroplasticity include
  • Using neural pathways is key to making them stronger and easier to access
  • Repeatition and repeated exposure is key to reinforcing neural pathways
  • New and varied experiences can help with creating new pathways and strengthening existing pathways
  • Eating well, exercise, and sleep can impact neuroplasticity
  • Making connections between things you already know to reinforce neural pathways

  • Moving my body, eating well, resting and sleeping are important for maintaining neuroplasticity
  • Making a commitment to practice new skills
  • Understand that habits are built through reinforcement, positive or negative, such as positive self talk and motivation
What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?
  • Practicing and repeptitive habits to strengthen neural pathways
  • Putting new learned skills into context of your life or analogies to connect them with established pathways

Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

A growth mindset is a way of thinking and approaching challenges that emphasizes the abilities and intelligence can be developed through hard work, dedication, and perseverance. Growth mindset views failures and setbacks as learning opportunities and growth, rather than limitations or shortcomings.

Growth mindset is relevant here because it requires a constant willingness to learn and adapt. Being open minded about change and learning is key to adpating to new challenges and techniques. Having a growth mindset is more more like to approach challenges with a positive attitude, viewing them as opportunities for growth and development and more like to persist in the face of difficult problems, seeking out solutions and new strategies to overcome obstacles.

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

Learning about the concept of growth mindset has helped me to re-frame my approach to learning. It was surprising to learn that my ability and intelligence isn't fixed. Before starting this course, I thought my abilities were fixed and that receiving compliments about my work was the only way to confirm that I was learning and capable. However, understanding the principles of growth mindset has helped me to adopt a more positive attitude towards struggling and failing.

Even just learning about it, I'm more willing to view challenges and setbacks as opportunities for growth and improvement, rather than as evidence of my limitations. Now when I encounter difficulties, I am more curious about why I am struggling and open to asking for help. I am more willing to take risks and try new approaches, knowing that learning is a process that requires effort and perseverance.
