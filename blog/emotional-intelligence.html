Emotional Intelligence

Monday, 10th April 2023

Question: How is EQ different to IQ?

IQ is a tool for measuring your intellectiual abilities such as cognitive skills, reasoning, logic and problem solving.

Cognative skills the core skills your brain uses to think, read, learn, remember, reason, and pay attention

Reasoning is the action of thinking about something in a logical, sensible way.

Problem Solving The act of defining the problem, and implimenting solutions

EQ is the ability to manage both your own emotions and understand the emotions of people around you using tools such as self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, and social skills

Self awareness Being conscious of ones own character and feelings. Being aware of your strengths, limitations and actions. Bodily self awareness (related proprioception), emotional self awareness, social self awareness

Self-regulation Control your emotions, actions and words. Goal setting, self-monotoring, self-talk and self reinforcement. Identify and reduce triggers, use bodily self awareness, be mindful of how you talk to your self, use positive self talk (affirmations), look for positive emtions and choose your words wisely.

Empathy

Ability to understand and share the feelings of another. Cognative empathy, emotional empathy and other types. Emotional/affective empathy - ability to respond appropriately to anothers mental state - empathic concern. Cognative empathy - ability to understand anothers perspective / mental state - perspective taking, tactical empathy and emotional regulation.

Motivation Ability of personal drive to improve and achieve and have a commitment to our goals, initiative, readieness to act on opportunities. Optimism and resilience.

Social SkillsAbility to properly manage your own and other peoples emotions, to connect and interact with others.

Question: Why is EQ important?

Emotional Intelligence is foundational to all your relationships from friends, to work, to family. It helps you build meaningful connections, relate to others and use effective conflict resolution. It also helps to better understand yourself, how to manage your emotions from the stress at work, to your relationships. Phil stutz defines there are 3 aspects of reality that noone can avoid, Pain uncertainty and constant work. Emotional intelligence helps us manage those aspects of reality in ways that make existance more bearable.
